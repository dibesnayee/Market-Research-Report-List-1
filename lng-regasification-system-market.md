<p><p>The LNG regasification system market is categorized into two main types: on-land and floating barges. On-land regasification facilities are fixed installations typically located near coastal areas, designed to convert liquefied natural gas back into its gaseous form for distribution. In contrast, floating regasification units (FRUs) are mobile installations on barges or ships that provide flexibility in location, allowing for quick deployment in diverse settings. Both types cater to specific logistical and infrastructural requirements, enhancing natural gas availability and supply chain efficiency.</p></p>
