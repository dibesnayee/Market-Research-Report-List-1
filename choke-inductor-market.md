<p><ul><li>Self-Inductor</li><li>Mutual Inductor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The choke inductor market comprises two main types: self-inductors and mutual inductors. Self-inductors are components that store energy in a magnetic field created by their own coil, primarily used for filtering applications in power supplies. On the other hand, mutual inductors consist of two or more coils that influence each other’s magnetic fields, often utilized in transformers and coupling applications. Both types are essential in managing electrical currents, ensuring stable performance in various electronic devices.</p></p>
