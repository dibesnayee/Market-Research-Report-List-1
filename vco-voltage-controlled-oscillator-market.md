<p><p>A Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO) is an electronic circuit that produces a frequency output that varies with an input voltage. The market comprises mainly two types: Quartz Oscillators and Silicon Oscillators. Quartz Oscillators use quartz crystals for high stability and precision, commonly used in telecommunications and computing. Silicon Oscillators, on the other hand, leverage silicon technology for lower costs and integration flexibility, making them suitable for consumer electronics and various industrial applications. Both types play crucial roles in frequency synthesis and modulation systems.</p></p>
