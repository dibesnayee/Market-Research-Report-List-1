<p><p>The cranial molding helmet market comprises two main types: active helmets and passive helmets. Active helmets are designed with adjustable components that actively reshape the skull by exerting controlled pressure, promoting optimal cranial development. In contrast, passive helmets provide a more static approach, allowing the head to grow naturally while gently guiding its shape without active adjustments. Both types aim to address conditions like plagiocephaly, supporting healthy cranial growth in infants through different methodologies and levels of intervention.</p></p>
